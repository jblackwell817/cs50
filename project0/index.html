About me

While often associated with Ireland, stouts are said to have originally derived from porters in 18th Century London. These days it has a wide range of sub-styles including (but far from limited to) Milk Stouts, Oatmeal Stouts, Baltic Porters, Oyster Stouts and Imperial Stouts.

Originally, porters were made using large quantities of brown malt instead of the more common pale malt. However, when this became too expensive recipes were adapted to use mostly pale malt with some additonal black malt. Hop content tends to be fairly low, and stouts get a lot of their taste from the malt itself, and from the different ways in which the malt has been kilned. In general, I am a fan of a sweeter stout but I enjoy the huge variety currently available.
